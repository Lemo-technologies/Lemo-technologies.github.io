Topic: MOSFET Switching & The Miller Plateau (Power Electronics).

In power electronics, particularly when building an induction heater (like your ZVS driver), we often model the MOSFET as a perfect switch‚Äîeither ON or OFF. This is a dangerous simplification. The reality is that the transition state involves complex parasitic capacitance.

The Phenomenon: When you apply voltage to the Gate, the MOSFET begins to turn on. However, between the Gate and the Drain, there exists a tiny, invisible capacitor called the Miller Capacitor ($C_{gd}$). As the Drain voltage starts to plummet (as the switch closes), it couples through this capacitor back to the Gate.

This negative feedback loop fights the Gate driver. The current you are pumping into the Gate stops raising the Gate voltage and is instead used entirely to charge/discharge this parasitic capacitor. On an oscilloscope, this looks like a flat line in the voltage rise‚Äîthe "Miller Plateau."

The Mathematical Danger:
Power loss is defined as Voltage times Current. Ideally, when Current is high, Voltage is zero (ON). When Voltage is high, Current is zero (OFF). But during the plateau, both are high.

$$P_{loss} = \int_{t_{on}}^{t_{off}} v_{ds}(t) \cdot i_d(t) \, dt$$

If $V_{ds} = 300V$ and $I_d = 20A$, the instantaneous power dissipation during that microsecond is 6,000 Watts. This creates a massive thermal spike that can explode the silicon die.

The Fix: You cannot remove the capacitance (it is physical). You must overcome it. Use a Gate Driver capable of sourcing high peak currents (2A - 5A) to charge $C_{gd}$ as fast as possible, minimizing the time spent in the plateau.

Topic: The Skin Effect (Deep Dive into AC Transmission).

We often assume current uses the entire cross-section of a wire, like water flowing through a pipe. This is false for Alternating Current (AC). As frequency increases, the current is pushed to the outer "skin" of the conductor, leaving the core dead and useless. The Physics: An alternating current creates an alternating magnetic field inside the conductor itself. By Lenz's Law, this magnetic field induces "eddy currents" in the center of the wire that flow in the opposite direction to the main current, cancelling it out. The current is forced to the edges where the opposition is weakest.

$$\delta = \sqrt{\frac{2\rho}{\omega\mu}}$$

Where $\rho$ is resistivity, $\omega$ is angular frequency, and $\mu$ is permeability. At 50Hz, the skin depth is about 8.5mm. At 1 MHz (radio frequency), it is microscopic.

Application: This is why high-voltage transmission lines are often made of stranded aluminum (ACSR) with a steel core. We don't need copper in the middle because no current flows there! Law Enforcement

Topic: The Burden of Proof (Onus Probandi).

In any legal system, "he who alleges must prove." The accused does not need to prove innocence; the state must prove guilt. But the standard changes depending on the court.

1. Criminal Court: "Beyond Reasonable Doubt." This is a high bar (approx. 99% certainty). It is better to let 10 guilty men go free than convict 1 innocent man.
2. Civil Court: "Balance of Probabilities." This is a lower bar (51% certainty). Whose version is more likely true?
Lesson: This is why O.J. Simpson was acquitted of murder (Criminal) but found liable for damages (Civil). The evidence was strong enough for 51%, but not 99%.
