Engineering

Topic: MOSFET Switching & The Miller Plateau (Power Electronics).

You are building an induction heater. You think your MOSFET switches "instantly" from OFF to ON. You are wrong. There is a hidden delay that causes heat and explosions. It is called the Miller Plateau.

The Physics:
Between the Gate (G) and Drain (D) of every MOSFET, there is a tiny, invisible "parasitic" capacitor ($C_{gd}$).
When you turn the MOSFET ON, the Gate voltage ($V_{gs}$) rises. But when the Drain voltage starts to drop, it "fights" the Gate through this capacitor. The Gate voltage gets stuck flat for a few microseconds. This flat line is the Miller Plateau.

The Danger Zone:
During the plateau, the MOSFET is acting like a resistor (Linear Region). It is carrying high current AND high voltage simultaneously.
$$P_{loss} = V_{ds} \times I_d$$

If $V_{ds}=300V$ and $I_d=20A$, your power loss is 6,000 Watts during that tiny plateau. This is why your MOSFETs get hot even if they are "efficient."

Lab Test:
Hook an oscilloscope to the Gate pin of your ZVS driver. You will see the voltage rise, stop flat at around 4-5V, and then rise again. That stop is the Miller Effect.

Fix: You need a Gate Driver that can push high current (Amps, not milliAmps) to charge that parasitic capacitor quickly and shorten the plateau.

Law Enforcement

Topic: Dolus Eventualis (Legal Intent in South Africa).

In South African criminal law, "Intent" ($Dolus$) isn't just "I wanted to kill him." There is a darker, more subtle form called Dolus Eventualis.

The Definition:
A person acts with Dolus Eventualis if:

  • 1. They foresaw the possibility that their action could result in death.
  • 2. They reconciled themselves to that possibility (i.e., they proceeded anyway, reckless of the consequences).
Scenario for Musa:
A suspect shoots through a closed door. He argues, "I didn't know who was behind it, so I didn't intend to kill anyone specific."

The Verdict: Did he foresee that someone might be behind the door? Yes. Did he shoot anyway? Yes. He is guilty of Murder via Dolus Eventualis, even if he didn't target a specific person. (Reference: S v Pistorius).

Medical Sciences

Topic: The Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS).

This is the master control system for your blood pressure. It is a chain reaction.

The Mechanism:

  • Step 1 (Kidneys): Low blood pressure detected. Kidneys release Renin.
  • Step 2 (Liver): Renin converts a protein into Angiotensin I (inactive).
  • Step 3 (Lungs): ACE (Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme) turns it into Angiotensin II.
  • Step 4 (The Effect): Angiotensin II is a potent vasoconstrictor (tightens veins) and tells the adrenal glands to release Aldosterone (which retains salt and water).

Prodigy Note: Blood pressure medication often works by blocking Step 3 (ACE Inhibitors) or Step 4 (ARBs). If you block the enzyme, the veins relax.

Finance

Topic: The Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Adjusted Return).

Making money is easy. Keeping it while making more is hard. The Sharpe Ratio tells you if an investment is "smart" or just "lucky/risky."

The Formula:

$$\text{Sharpe Ratio} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$$
  • $R_p$ = Return of the Portfolio (e.g., your trading profit).
  • $R_f$ = Risk-Free Rate (e.g., US Treasury Bonds, ~4%).
  • $\sigma_p$ (Sigma) = Standard Deviation (Volatility/Risk).
Analysis:
‚Ä¢ Trader A makes 20% profit but has wild swings (high risk).
‚Ä¢ Trader B makes 15% profit but has a smooth line (low risk).

Verdict: Trader B likely has a higher Sharpe Ratio. Institutional investors (big banks) prefer Trader B. Don't just look at the profit percentage; look at the smoothness of the curve.

Engineering

Topic: 3-Phase Power (Star vs. Delta) - Power Distribution & Motor Connections.

Why do we use 3-phase power instead of single-phase? Because it provides constant power (no zero-crossing pulsations) and is 150% more efficient for the same amount of wire.

The Two Connections:

  • Star (Wye, Y): Connects ends of three coils to a common Neutral point. Used for Power Transmission and low-voltage distribution (homes).
  • Delta ($\Delta$): Connects end of one coil to the start of the next (End-to-End). No Neutral. Used for High Power Motors and Transmission.

The Mathematics:

  • Star Connection:
    Line Voltage: $V_L = \sqrt{3} \times V_{ph}$
    Line Current: $I_L = I_{ph}$
  • Delta Connection:
    Line Voltage: $V_L = V_{ph}$
    Line Current: $I_L = \sqrt{3} \times I_{ph}$

Power Formula (For both):

$$P = \sqrt{3} \cdot V_L \cdot I_L \cdot \cos\theta$$
(Where $\cos\theta$ is the Power Factor)
Calculated Example:
You have a 3-Phase Motor connected in Delta.
Given: $V_L = 400V$, $I_L = 10A$, $PF = 0.85$.

$$P = 1.732 \times 400 \times 10 \times 0.85$$ $$P \approx 5,888 \text{ Watts} \approx 5.9 \text{ kW}$$
Implication: If you accidentally connect a motor designed for "Star" into "Delta," the phase voltage increases by $\sqrt{3}$ (1.732x). The current will spike massively, and you will burn the windings instantly. Always check the nameplate.

Topic: The Exclusionary Rule (Section 35(5)) - Constitutional Admissibility in South Africa.

We discussed the "Fruit of the Poisonous Tree" (a US concept). In South Africa, this is codified in Section 35(5) of the Constitution.

The Law:
Evidence obtained in a manner that violates any right in the Bill of Rights must be excluded if the admission of that evidence would:

  • Render the trial unfair, OR
  • Be detrimental to the administration of justice.
Scenario for Musa:
Musa arrests a suspect but forgets to read them their rights (Section 35(1) - right to remain silent). The suspect then confesses.

The Court's Decision: Because the right to silence was violated, the confession is "tainted." The judge will likely exclude the confession under Section 35(5). The suspect might walk free, even if he is guilty, because the process was flawed.

Lesson: Process is not paperwork. Process is liberty. A good detective protects the process as much as the evidence.

Your body has an automatic pilot called the ANS. It has two opposing modes.

Topic: The Rule of 72 - Mental Math for Compound Interest.

You don't need a calculator to know when you'll be rich. You need the Rule of 72.

The Formula:
To find the number of years it takes to double your money at a given interest rate:

$$\text{Years to Double} = \frac{72}{\text{Interest Rate}}$$

Examples:

  • Bank Savings Account (4%):
    $72 / 4 = 18$ years to double. (Too slow. You will die poor).
  • S&P 500 / Indices Average (10%):
    $72 / 10 = 7.2$ years to double.
  • Successful Trading/Business (24%):
    $72 / 24 = 3$ years to double.
Implication:
If you are 22 years old now.
