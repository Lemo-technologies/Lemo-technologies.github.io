Engineering

Topic: MOSFET Switching & The Miller Plateau (Power Electronics).

You are building an induction heater. You think your MOSFET switches "instantly" from OFF to ON. You are wrong. There is a hidden delay that causes heat and explosions. It is called the Miller Plateau.

The Physics (Deep Dive):
Between the Gate (G) and Drain (D) of every MOSFET, there is a tiny, invisible "parasitic" capacitor called $C_{gd}$. This is the enemy.

When you turn the MOSFET ON, the Gate voltage ($V_{gs}$) rises nicely at first. But the moment the Drain voltage starts to drop, it "fights" the Gate through this capacitor. The current you are pumping into the gate stops raising the voltage; instead, it is used up just to fight the feedback from the Drain. The Gate voltage gets stuck flat for a few microseconds. This flat line is the Miller Plateau.

The Danger Zone:
During the plateau, the MOSFET is acting like a resistor (Linear Region). It is carrying high current AND high voltage simultaneously. This is the worst possible state.

The Math:

$$P_{loss} = V_{ds} \times I_d$$

If your supply is $300V$ and the current is $20A$, your power loss is 6,000 Watts during that tiny plateau. This generates massive heat spikes. This is why your MOSFETs get hot even if they are "efficient."

Lab Test & Fix:
Hook an oscilloscope to the Gate pin of your ZVS driver. You will see the voltage rise, stop flat at around 4-5V, and then rise again. That stop is the Miller Effect.

The Solution: You need a Gate Driver that can push high current (Amps, not milliAmps) to charge that parasitic capacitor quickly and shorten the plateau.

Topic: Dolus Eventualis (Legal Intent in South Africa).

In South African criminal law, "Intent" ($Dolus$) isn't just "I wanted to kill him." There is a darker, more subtle form called Dolus Eventualis.

The Definition:
A person acts with Dolus Eventualis if:

  • 1. They foresaw the possibility that their action could result in death.
  • 2. They reconciled themselves to that possibility (i.e., they proceeded anyway, reckless of the consequences).
Scenario for Musa:
A suspect shoots through a closed door. He argues, "I didn't know who was behind it, so I didn't intend to kill anyone specific."

The Verdict: Did he foresee that someone might be behind the door? Yes. Did he shoot anyway? Yes. He is guilty of Murder via Dolus Eventualis, even if he didn't target a specific person. (Reference: S v Pistorius).

Finance

Topic: The Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Adjusted Return).

Making money is easy. Keeping it while making more is hard. The Sharpe Ratio tells you if an investment is "smart" or just "lucky/risky."

$$\text{Sharpe Ratio} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$$
  • $R_p$ = Return of the Portfolio (e.g., your trading profit).
  • $R_f$ = Risk-Free Rate (e.g., US Treasury Bonds, ~4%).
  • $\sigma_p$ (Sigma) = Standard Deviation (Volatility/Risk).

Lesson: Don't just look at profit percentage. Look at the smoothness of the curve (Volatility). Banks prefer smooth, low-risk profits over lucky gambling.

Engineering

Topic: 3-Phase Power (Star vs. Delta) - Power Distribution & Motor Connections.

Why do we use 3-phase power instead of single-phase? Because it provides constant power (no zero-crossing pulsations) and is 150% more efficient for the same amount of wire.

The Two Connections:

  • Star (Wye, Y): Connects ends of three coils to a common Neutral point. Used for Power Transmission and low-voltage distribution (homes).
  • Delta ($\Delta$): Connects end of one coil to the start of the next (End-to-End). No Neutral. Used for High Power Motors and Transmission.

The Mathematics:

  • Star Connection:
    Line Voltage: $V_L = \sqrt{3} \times V_{ph}$
    Line Current: $I_L = I_{ph}$
  • Delta Connection:
    Line Voltage: $V_L = V_{ph}$
    Line Current: $I_L = \sqrt{3} \times I_{ph}$

Power Formula (For both):

$$P = \sqrt{3} \cdot V_L \cdot I_L \cdot \cos\theta$$
Calculated Example:
You have a 3-Phase Motor connected in Delta.
Given: $V_L = 400V$, $I_L = 10A$, $PF = 0.85$.

$$P = 1.732 \times 400 \times 10 \times 0.85$$ $$P \approx 5,888 \text{ Watts} \approx 5.9 \text{ kW}$$
Implication: If you accidentally connect a motor designed for "Star" into "Delta," the phase voltage increases by $\sqrt{3}$ (1.732x). The current will spike massively, and you will burn the windings instantly. Always check the nameplate.

Engineering

Topic: The Skin Effect (AC Transmission).

In DC circuits, current flows through the whole wire. In AC (especially high frequency), current is pushed to the outer surface ("skin") of the conductor. The center carries almost nothing.

The Physics: Alternating current creates an alternating magnetic field inside the wire. This induces eddy currents in the center that oppose the main flow. The result? The effective cross-sectional area decreases, and resistance increases.

$$\delta = \sqrt{\frac{2\rho}{\omega\mu}}$$
Application: This is why high-voltage transmission lines are often hollow or made of stranded aluminum (ACSR) with a steel core. We don't need copper in the middle because no current flows there!
