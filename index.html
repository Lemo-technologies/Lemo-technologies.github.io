Engineering

Topic: MOSFET Switching & The Miller Plateau (Power Electronics).

In power electronics, particularly when building an induction heater (like your ZVS driver), we often model the MOSFET as a perfect switch—either ON or OFF. This is a dangerous simplification. The reality is that the transition state involves complex parasitic capacitance.

The Phenomenon: When you apply voltage to the Gate, the MOSFET begins to turn on. However, between the Gate and the Drain, there exists a tiny, invisible capacitor called the Miller Capacitor ($C_{gd}$). As the Drain voltage starts to plummet (as the switch closes), it couples through this capacitor back to the Gate.

This negative feedback loop fights the Gate driver. The current you are pumping into the Gate stops raising the Gate voltage and is instead used entirely to charge/discharge this parasitic capacitor. On an oscilloscope, this looks like a flat line in the voltage rise—the "Miller Plateau."

The Mathematical Danger:
Power loss is defined as Voltage times Current. Ideally, when Current is high, Voltage is zero (ON). When Voltage is high, Current is zero (OFF). But during the plateau, both are high.

$$P_{loss} = \int_{t_{on}}^{t_{off}} v_{ds}(t) \cdot i_d(t) \, dt$$

If $V_{ds} = 300V$ and $I_d = 20A$, the instantaneous power dissipation during that microsecond is 6,000 Watts. This creates a massive thermal spike that can explode the silicon die.

The Fix: You cannot remove the capacitance (it is physical). You must overcome it. Use a Gate Driver capable of sourcing high peak currents (2A - 5A) to charge $C_{gd}$ as fast as possible, minimizing the time spent in the plateau.

Topic: Dolus Eventualis (Legal Intent).

In South African criminal law, intent ($Dolus$) is not binary. The most complex form is Dolus Eventualis.

The Definition: A perpetrator acts with Dolus Eventualis if:

  1. They subjectively foresaw the possibility that their act could result in the unlawful consequence (e.g., death).
  2. They reconciled themselves to that possibility (i.e., they proceeded anyway, reckless of the outcome).
Scenario for Musa: A suspect shoots through a closed bathroom door. He claims, "I didn't know who was behind it, so I didn't intend to kill anyone specific."

Verdict: The court asks: Did he foresee that someone might be behind the door? Yes. Did he shoot anyway? Yes. He is guilty of Murder via Dolus Eventualis. (Reference: S v Pistorius).

Finance

Topic: The Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Adjusted Return).

Amateur traders look at returns (%). Professional fund managers look at the Sharpe Ratio. It answers the question: "Is this profit due to skill, or did you just take a massive risk?"

$$\text{Sharpe Ratio} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$$

Where $\sigma_p$ (Sigma) is the Standard Deviation (Volatility). A high return with high volatility results in a low Sharpe Ratio. A moderate return with almost zero volatility (a straight line up) results in a high Sharpe Ratio. This is what banks buy.

Engineering

Topic: 3-Phase Power (Star vs. Delta).

Why do we use 3-phase power? Because it provides constant power transfer (no zero-crossing pulsations) and is 150% more efficient for the same amount of wire conductor material compared to single-phase.

The Two Topologies:

  • Star (Wye, Y): Connects the ends of three coils to a common Neutral point. Used for Power Transmission and low-voltage distribution because it offers two voltages (Phase and Line).
    Math: $V_{Line} = \sqrt{3} \times V_{Phase}$ and $I_{Line} = I_{Phase}$.
  • Delta ($\Delta$): Connects the end of one coil to the start of the next. No Neutral. Used for high-power motors and transmission.
    Math: $V_{Line} = V_{Phase}$ and $I_{Line} = \sqrt{3} \times I_{Phase}$.
$$P = \sqrt{3} \cdot V_L \cdot I_L \cdot \cos\theta$$
Calculated Example: You have a 3-Phase Motor connected in Delta.
Given: $V_L = 400V$, $I_L = 10A$, $PF = 0.85$.
Calculation: $1.732 \times 400 \times 10 \times 0.85 \approx 5,888$ Watts.
Warning: If you accidentally connect a motor designed for "Star" into "Delta," the voltage across the windings increases by 1.732x. The current will spike, and the motor will burn out instantly.

Engineering

Topic: The Skin Effect (Deep Dive into AC Transmission).

We often assume current uses the entire cross-section of a wire, like water flowing through a pipe. This is false for Alternating Current (AC). As frequency increases, the current is pushed to the outer "skin" of the conductor, leaving the core dead and useless. This effectively reduces the usable cross-sectional area and drastically increases resistance.

The Physics: An alternating current creates an alternating magnetic field inside the conductor itself. By Lenz's Law, this magnetic field induces "eddy currents" in the center of the wire that flow in the opposite direction to the main current, cancelling it out. The current is forced to the edges where the opposition is weakest.

$$\delta = \sqrt{\frac{2\rho}{\omega\mu}}$$

Where $\rho$ is resistivity, $\omega$ is angular frequency, and $\mu$ is permeability. At 50Hz, the skin depth is about 8.5mm. At 1 MHz (radio frequency), it is microscopic.

Application: This is why high-voltage transmission lines are often made of stranded aluminum (ACSR) with a steel core. We don't need copper in the middle because no current flows there! In induction heating (your project), we use Litz Wire—thousands of insulated strands woven together to maximize surface area and defeat the Skin Effect.

Law Enforcement

Topic: The Burden of Proof (Onus Probandi).

In any legal system, "he who alleges must prove." The accused does not need to prove innocence; the state must prove guilt. But the standard changes depending on the court.

1. Criminal Court: "Beyond Reasonable Doubt." This is a high bar (approx. 99% certainty). It is better to let 10 guilty men go free than convict 1 innocent man.
2. Civil Court: "Balance of Probabilities." This is a lower bar (51% certainty). Whose version is more likely true?
Lesson: This is why O.J. Simpson was acquitted of murder (Criminal) but found liable for damages (Civil). The evidence was strong enough for 51%, but not 99%.
